Executive Summary¶

Objective¶

The objective of this project was to analyze datasets related to a Food Delivery App, including restaurant and order datasets, to uncover patterns and insights that can drive business improvements. Emphasis was placed on mastering the structured analytical process: selecting key business questions, performing thorough data cleaning, and conducting Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA). Specifically, the analysis aimed to understand the relationship between order characteristics (such as order amount, delivery time, and cuisine type) and customer satisfaction, with the goal of informing strategies to enhance service quality, boost orders, and ultimately increase revenue.

Methodology¶

Python was used for data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and visualization. The analysis included grouping data by various factors, calculating averages, and exploring relationships between different variables. Key tools and libraries include Pandas for data manipulation, Seaborn for visualization, and Matplotlib for plotting.

Key Findings and Recommendations¶

Order Amounts and Cuisine

  • Finding: The analysis shows consistent spending patterns across different cuisines, with slight variations suggesting targeted promotions could boost sales for specific cuisines like African or Chinese, which tend to have higher average order amounts.
  • Recommendation: Leverage the consistency in spending patterns by promoting cuisines with higher order amounts during targeted campaigns to increase sales, while also experimenting with promotions for less popular cuisines to see if they can be uplifted.

Customer Satisfaction and Delivery Time

  • Finding: Shorter delivery times generally correlate with higher satisfaction, but the relationship is complex. Some longer delivery times also received relatively high ratings, suggesting that other factors like food quality or communication might influence satisfaction levels.
  • Recommendation: Focus on reducing delivery times during peak hours to enhance customer satisfaction. Additionally, consider investigating other factors such as communication during delays or the overall quality of the delivered product to improve satisfaction further.

Popular Cuisines and Restaurants

  • Finding: Some cuisines and restaurants consistently received higher orders, indicating a trend in customer preferences, particularly towards African and Belgian cuisines.
  • Recommendation: Implement direct feedback mechanisms to understand why certain cuisines and restaurants are more popular, and use this information to refine menu offerings and marketing strategies to better align with customer preferences. Consider targeted marketing campaigns for less popular cuisines to boost their demand.

3.2. Outlier Detection¶

This section involves checking for potential outliers in the numerical columns of the Orders dataset using the Interquartile Range (IQR) method. Outliers may indicate data entry errors or represent rare but valid events. Identifying them is crucial for ensuring data accuracy and reliability.

Function to calculate and return outliers:
A function 'detect_outliers_iqr' is defined to calculate the interquartile range (IQR) for a given column and identify values that fall outside of 1.5 times the IQR from the first (Q1) and third quartile (Q3). These values are considered potential outliers.

In [63]:
# Function to calculate and return outliers in a column using the IQR method
def detect_outliers_iqr(df, column):
    Q1 = df[column].quantile(0.25)
    Q3 = df[column].quantile(0.75)
    IQR = Q3 - Q1
    lower_bound = Q1 - 1.5 * IQR
    upper_bound = Q3 + 1.5 * IQR
    outliers = df[(df[column] < lower_bound) | (df[column] > upper_bound)]
    return outliers

4.1.3. Findings Documentation¶

Findings
From the data, it appears that Debit Card is the most frequently used payment method, followed closely by Cash on Delivery and then Credit Card.

Business Implications
Given that Debit Card and Cash on Delivery are popular, the business might consider focusing on optimizing these payment methods. For example:

  • Ensuring the Debit Card payment process is smooth and secure, as this is the most used method.
  • Streamlining the Cash on Delivery process to minimize delays and errors.

The company might consider targeted promotions or incentives for using less popular payment methods like Credit Cards, to balance the usage across different methods.

Limitations

  • Small Dataset: The analysis is based on a relatively small dataset of 500 records. As a result, the differences in payment method usage might not be statistically significant. In a real-world scenario with a larger dataset, the results might differ.
  • Lack of Contextual Data: The dataset does not provide information about the reasons behind the payment method choices, such as user preferences, regional differences, or economic factors, which could impact the results.
  • Potential Sampling Bias: If this data is not representative of the entire customer base, the conclusions drawn may not generalize to all customers.

Recommendations

  • Further Analysis with Larger Dataset: To draw more reliable conclusions, it would be beneficial to perform the same analysis on a larger and more diverse dataset.
  • User Experience Survey: Conducting surveys or interviews with customers could provide insights into why they prefer certain payment methods, which could further inform business strategies.
